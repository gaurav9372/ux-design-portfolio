## story-p1
I got into design the way most people do. By accident. I was building a small project for a friend's business and realized I cared more about how the thing felt to use than how it looked. That was the moment it clicked. I did not want to make things pretty. I wanted to make things work.

## story-p2
Today I design across web and mobile, mostly for startups and small product teams. E-commerce checkouts, SaaS dashboards, healthcare tools, fintech apps. The kind of stuff where a bad experience costs real money and a good one builds real trust. I like projects that have constraints because that is where the interesting decisions happen.

## story-p3
What makes me different is probably my process. I do not jump into Figma on day one. I start by talking to people, understanding the problem, and mapping out the experience before I draw a single rectangle. I also work closely with developers because the best designs are the ones that actually get built the way they were intended.

## value-1-title
Research before rectangles

## value-1-body
I do not open Figma until I understand who I am designing for and why. Assumptions are expensive. Conversations with real users are not.

## value-2-title
Constraints are direction

## value-2-body
Tight deadlines and technical limits are not problems. They tell me where to focus. Some of my best work came from projects with the most limitations.

## value-3-title
Ship and learn

## value-3-body
A good design shipped today beats a perfect design shipped never. I design for iteration, not perfection. You can always improve version two.

## value-4-title
Developers are design partners

## value-4-body
My best work happens when I sit with the people who build what I design. Not after the handoff. During the process.
